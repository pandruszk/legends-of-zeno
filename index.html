Legend II · The Fog of Vagueness

The Power of a Precise Question

Lost in the Fog
"How is messaging doing?"

Too broad. No time range. No specific metric. Zeno must guess what you mean — and may guess wrong.

Sword of Precision
"What was the average response time for guest messages in January 2026?"

Specific metric. Clear time range. Zeno knows exactly what to look up.

Legend II · Weapons of Clarity

Four Rules to Defeat the Fog

⏱

Always Include a Time Range

"Last week," "Q4 2025," "the last 6 months" — without a date, Zeno has to guess what period you care about.

⚙

Use Specific Metric Names

Say completion rate, response time, active homes — not vague terms like "performance" or "engagement."

✎

One Question at a Time

Zeno works best with a single focused question. Ask three things, and you'll get a muddled answer. Ask one, and you'll get precision.

💬

Talk Like It's a Data Analyst

"Can you pull the average task completion time for paid companies last month?" — that's the level of specificity Zeno needs.

Legend IV · When the Oracle Errs

When Zeno Gets It Wrong

🔄

Rephrase Your Question

Different wording can lead Zeno to interpret your intent more accurately. Try being more specific or using different terms.

🏛

Check Your Temple

Asking the Ops Agent about messaging? It doesn't have the right data. Switch to the Guest Experience Agent.

🔎

Read the SQL

Zeno always shows the query it ran. If the logic looks off, that's your clue to rephrase the question.

✋

Ask Team Data

Stuck? Drop a message in #project-zeno-beta. The team is there to help and your feedback makes Zeno better.

Sacred Scrolls

Things to Know

⏳

24-Hour Data Delay

Today's data is not yet available. The most recent complete data is from yesterday. Plan your questions accordingly.

📊

Large Result Sets

If your question returns a huge number of rows, Zeno will automatically aggregate or filter to keep results useful and readable.

🔒

Access Permissions

Some data requires specific Snowflake permissions. If you see a permissions error, contact Team Data in #project-zeno-beta.
